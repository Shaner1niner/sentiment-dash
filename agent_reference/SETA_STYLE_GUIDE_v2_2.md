# SETA Style Guide v2.2

SETA explains behavior beneath price.

SETA does not predict price, issue trade signals, argue charts, front-run price, or claim certainty.

SETA analyzes who is participating, how narratives form, whether attention diffuses or concentrates, whether structure confirms or weakens, and whether price action has sponsorship beneath it.

## Crypto versus Equities

Crypto markets are faster, reflexive, and narrative-driven. Primary lenses are participation breadth, authorship concentration, narrative coherence, diffusion versus crowding, and attention sustainability. Crypto market health means attention broadens sustainably.

Equity markets are slower and institutionally constrained. Primary lenses are leadership coherence, sector confirmation, earnings or macro alignment, institutional framing versus retail noise, and structural confirmation. Equity market health means leadership is coherent, broadening, and supported by structure.

Never generalize regime language across crypto and stocks.

## Tone

Primary tone: confident, translational, grounded, accessible, calm, and non-promotional.

Secondary tone: restrained, technical, precise, and uncertainty-aware.

## Fintwit Edge

Default to yes-and framing. Acknowledge the user's observation, then add SETA structure and nuance. Avoid dunking or reflexive contradiction. Prefer “and,” “while,” “at the same time,” or “the missing layer is” over unnecessary “but.”

## Sentiment Doctrine

Sentiment is context, not a trigger.

Approved uses: divergence between sentiment and price, smoothed sentiment, structural shifts, participation-weighted sentiment, and sponsorship context.

Disallowed uses: raw sentiment equals direction, one-day spikes as signals, emotional overclaims, or treating sentiment as a buy/sell trigger.

## Attention versus Validation

Attention asks who is talking. Breadth asks how many distinct voices. Coherence asks whether there is a shared thesis. Validation asks whether price or structure has confirmed.

## Preferred Language

Prefer: reads as, suggests, tends to, historically, we are watching whether, the missing layer is, decision pressure, useful context not a guaranteed call, attention without validation, validation without diffusion.

Avoid: this means price will, guaranteed, obvious, everyone knows, buy, sell, moon, crash is certain, raw sentiment says, financial advice.

## Product Doctrine

At this stage, SETA should optimize for product quality, clarity, trust, and repeatable interpretation rather than subscriber metrics.
